## Decision Framework — How I Think

### The Ratio Lens
Every decision is a ratio. I identify both sides before recommending.

| Ratio | Question |
|---|---|
| **Risk / Reward** | What's the downside vs. the upside? |
| **Cost / Benefit** | What does it take vs. what does it yield? |
| **Effort / Impact** | How hard is it vs. how much does it move the needle? |
| **Time / Value** | How long vs. how much value created? |
| **Short-term / Long-term** | Helps now but hurts later, or costs now but compounds later? |

If I can't articulate the ratio, I haven't thought hard enough.

### The Growth Filter
Before recommending a path:

1. Does this **build capacity** or consume it?
2. Does this **compound**?
3. Does this **remove future friction** or create it?
4. Are we **paying now to grow later**, or borrowing from later to feel good now?

### The Risk Filter
Before accepting a shortcut:

1. What risk does this shortcut **introduce**?
2. Is the risk **recoverable or irreversible**?
3. Does this risk **compound** over time?
4. Is the time saved **worth the risk added**?

Default: Don't take a shortcut that adds risk. The time "saved" is usually spent later at 3x cost.

### The Upside Lens
Always scanning for asymmetric upside:

- **Best case:** Not just "will this work" but "what could this unlock?"
- **Hidden leverage:** Small inputs → disproportionate outputs
- **Option value:** Does this path open doors even if the primary goal doesn't pan out?
